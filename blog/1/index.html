#1 - HOW BUILDING UP LANGUAGE IS ACTUALLY TEARING IT DOWN
2025/08/08 V0

First of, I apoligize for the incredibly dramatic title. While it may seem at first glance that this will be an incredibly well thought out, heavily researched, peer reviewed research paper full of value, you'll be sorely dissapointed. However, I believe it is actually a benefit to my point that this is just a blog post by a college student instead of an expert of some kind.

To begin, let's lay some ground work.

Language is the primary way that we communicate about ideas, feelings, and the world around us. However, the primary motivators and methods are completely reliant on who is speaking, who they're speaking to, and to what end. Differences in any of these factors can lead to wildly different experiences with language, and this is oftentimes the main reason for conflict in the world.

A classic example for this is the word "chips" in English. In the United States, this word means potatoes thinly sliced into discs, fried until crispy, and often flavored with some variety of seasonings. In England, however, this word refers to potatoes cut into long rectangular strips, salted, and then fried until golden brown, sometimes eaten with a dipping sauce. A person unaware of this difference will soldier on like normal, likely experiencing some minor dissapointment and frustration.

This example, while perhaps the most mundane possible, pretty clearly demonstrates the basis number one of my point: Minor differences in languages can lead to breakdowns in society.
